Why Free?

Built by Makers, for Makers

Ten years ago, I spent three months hunting down arcade cabinet plans. Every website wanted $50 for incomplete PDFs. Some were literal scans of 1980s manuals you couldn't even read. Others were "premium communities" that gatekept basic dimensions behind paywalls.

When I finally built my first cabinet, I measured everything, documented every cut, and photographed every mistake. That knowledge shouldn't cost money. The golden age of arcades was about communities gathering around machines, not about corporations extracting profit from nostalgia.

RcadePlayhu exists because arcade culture should be accessible to anyone with a weekend, some plywood, and the desire to build something with their hands. Every download, every shared build, every troubleshooting tip in our Discord is a middle finger to paywalls and a love letter to the maker spirit.

We're also doing our small part for the planet. Those old CRT monitors in your garage? They don't belong in landfills. They belong in a cabinet, glowing green with Pac-Man mazes, giving someone the same rush you felt dropping quarters in 1982.
